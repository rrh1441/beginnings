About Our Queen Anne Center

Nestled in the heart of Lower Queen Anne, our original location offers a warm, home-like setting with easy access for families commuting through downtown Seattle.

Founded in 1992 as a parent cooperative, this center carries on our community-driven roots. Many of our teachers have been with us for over a decade, creating lasting relationships with generations of Seattle families.

The renovated Victorian building features bright, airy classrooms with natural wood finishes and plenty of natural light. Our private outdoor play yard includes raised garden beds where children grow vegetables and herbs used in our kitchen.

What Makes Queen Anne Special

  • Cozy, renovated Victorian building with abundant natural light
  • Private outdoor play yard with garden beds children help tend
  • Average teacher tenure of 8+ years
  • Strong parent cooperative traditions with regular family events
  • Walking distance to Seattle Center and local parks
  • Convenient for downtown Seattle commuters

A Typical Day

Each day balances structure and flexibility, with time for exploration, rest, outdoor play, and connection.

7:00–9:00 AM Arrival, breakfast, and free play with morning activities
9:00–11:30 AM Circle time, project work, and outdoor play
11:30–12:30 PM Family-style lunch and transition to rest
12:30–3:00 PM Nap and quiet time (varies by age group)
3:00–4:00 PM Afternoon snack and small-group activities
4:00–6:00 PM Outdoor play, free choice, and gradual pickup
