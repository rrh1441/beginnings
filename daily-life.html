Meals & Daily Life

Every day at Beginnings balances structure and flexibility, with plenty of time for play, rest, outdoor exploration, and nourishing meals.

Nourishing Meals & Snacks

Good nutrition is foundational to learning and wellbeing. We take pride in the food we serve and the positive mealtime experiences we create.

All meals and snacks are prepared fresh in our kitchens using organic produce, whole grains, and minimally processed ingredients. We source locally when possible and plan menus that expose children to a variety of flavors and cuisines.

Organic & Whole Foods

Our menus emphasize organic produce, whole grains, and minimally processed ingredients. We avoid artificial colors, flavors, and excess sugar. A typical lunch might include roasted chicken, quinoa, steamed broccoli, and fresh fruit.

Inclusive of Dietary Needs

We accommodate allergies, intolerances, and family dietary preferences. Vegetarian, vegan, dairy-free, and other modifications are routine. Please share your child's needs during enrollment, and we'll work with you.

Family-Style Meals

Children serve themselves and eat together at small tables. This builds independence, fine motor skills, and social connection. Teachers eat with children, modeling manners and conversation.

A Typical Day

While every day brings new discoveries, our consistent rhythm helps children feel secure and know what to expect.

7:00–9:00 AM Arrival & Breakfast: Warm greetings, breakfast for early arrivals, and free play with morning activities like puzzles, books, and art.
9:00–9:30 AM Morning Gathering: Circle time with songs, stories, and discussion of the day's plans. A chance to connect as a group.
9:30–11:30 AM Learning Time & Outdoor Play: Small-group project work, art exploration, and extended outdoor play in our yards or nearby parks.
11:30–12:30 PM Lunch & Transition: Family-style lunch together, followed by bathroom routines and settling for rest.
12:30–3:00 PM Nap & Quiet Time: Restful sleep for younger children; quiet activities like books, puzzles, or drawing for older children who don't nap.
3:00–3:30 PM Wake Up & Snack: Gradual wake-up, afternoon snack, and transition to afternoon activities.
3:30–5:00 PM Afternoon Activities: More outdoor play, creative activities, dramatic play, and small-group exploration.
5:00–6:00 PM Wind Down & Pickup: Calm activities, stories, and gradual pickup. Teachers share highlights of your child's day.

Outdoor Play & Nature

Children spend significant time outdoors every day—rain or shine. Both of our locations have dedicated outdoor spaces designed for safe exploration and active play.

  • Age-appropriate climbing structures and gross motor challenges
  • Sand and water play areas
  • Garden beds where children grow vegetables and herbs
  • Natural elements like logs, rocks, and plants to explore
  • Regular walks to nearby parks and nature spots

We believe outdoor play is essential for physical development, sensory exploration, and connection to the natural world. Please send your child in weather-appropriate clothing—we go outside in all but the most extreme conditions.

Rest & Nap Time

Adequate rest is crucial for growing minds and bodies.

Infants

Infants nap on demand in individual cribs in a quiet sleep room. We follow safe sleep guidelines and coordinate with your home schedule as much as possible.

Toddlers

Toddlers nap after lunch on individual cots in a darkened room with soft music. Teachers provide comfort and help children settle. Typical naps are 1.5–2 hours.

Preschoolers

Preschoolers have a rest period after lunch. Children who sleep do so on cots; those who don't nap engage in quiet activities like reading, drawing, or puzzles.

Comfort Items

Children may bring a special blanket or stuffed animal for nap time. We keep these in their cubbies for use during rest.

Staying Connected

We know it's hard to be apart from your child. We prioritize communication so you always feel connected to their day.

Daily Updates via App

We use a secure family communication app to share real-time updates throughout the day. You'll see photos, activity notes, meal reports, and nap times—giving you a window into your child's day.

Pickup Conversations

Teachers are available at pickup for quick conversations about your child's day. We share highlights, observations, and anything we think you should know.

Developmental Updates

Beyond daily communication, teachers document children's learning and development through observations, photos, and work samples. We share these periodically and discuss during conferences.

Family Conferences

We schedule formal conferences twice a year to discuss your child's growth, development, and goals. Additional meetings are available anytime you have questions or concerns.

What to Bring

Daily Essentials

  • A complete change of weather-appropriate clothing
  • Weather-appropriate outerwear (rain jacket, warm layers)
  • Indoor shoes (slippers or soft-soled shoes)
  • Any necessary comfort items for nap

We Provide

  • All meals and snacks
  • Diapers and wipes (infants and toddlers)
  • Sunscreen and insect repellent
  • Cots, sheets, and blankets for nap
  • All art supplies and learning materials

For Infants

  • Labeled bottles (if breastfeeding or formula feeding)
  • Extra outfits (we go through more with babies!)
  • Special comfort items if needed

Please Label Everything

Label all clothing, bottles, and belongings with your child's name. Items get mixed up easily, and labels help us return everything to the right family.
