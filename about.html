About Beginnings Schools

For over 30 years, we've been nurturing Seattle's youngest learners through play-based, relationship-centered early education.

Our Mission

"We believe every child deserves a nurturing, play-rich environment where they are seen, valued, and supported in their unique developmental journey."

Who We Are

Beginnings Schools is a nonprofit early learning organization with deep roots in Seattle. We operate two NAEYC-accredited centers—one in Queen Anne and one in Capitol Hill—serving children from 3 months to 5 years old.

Founded in 1992 as a parent cooperative, we've grown while maintaining the community spirit that defined our beginning. Today, we blend the best of progressive early childhood education with practical support for working families.

As a 501(c)(3) nonprofit, every dollar we earn goes back into our programs, our teachers, and our facilities. We answer to our families and community—not to shareholders.

Our Educational Philosophy

Inspired by the Reggio Emilia approach, we view children as capable, curious, and full of potential.

Play-Based Learning

Children learn best through hands-on exploration and self-directed play. Our classrooms are designed as inviting studios where curiosity leads the way—not worksheets or rigid instruction. When children are engaged in meaningful play, they develop problem-solving skills, creativity, and intrinsic motivation.

Emergent Curriculum

Rather than following a fixed curriculum, our teachers observe children's interests and questions, then design learning experiences that extend their thinking. This approach honors children's natural curiosity and leads to deeper, more meaningful learning.

The Environment as Teacher

Our spaces are thoughtfully designed with natural materials, open-ended resources, and beautiful displays of children's work. The environment invites exploration and sends a message: you belong here, and your ideas matter.

Relationships at the Center

Strong bonds between children and caregivers are the foundation of healthy development. We prioritize continuity of care, small group sizes, and deep partnerships with families. When children feel secure, they're free to take risks and learn.

A Story from Our Classroom

"Last spring, our preschoolers became fascinated with a bird's nest they discovered in our playground tree. What started as simple observation turned into a months-long project.

The children sketched the nest daily, noticing changes. They researched what birds need to build nests and gathered materials. They measured and compared bird eggs to chicken eggs. They wrote stories from the bird's perspective. They invited a local ornithologist to visit and ask questions.

By the time the baby birds fledged, our preschoolers had practiced observation, measurement, writing, research, and scientific thinking—all driven by their genuine wonder. That's emergent curriculum in action."

— Fatima Al-Hassan
Lead Preschool Teacher, Queen Anne

Our Commitment to Inclusion

Every child and family is welcomed, valued, and celebrated at Beginnings.

Anti-Bias Education

We actively work to counter bias and stereotypes in our classrooms. Our curriculum includes diverse books, dolls, and materials that reflect all kinds of families, abilities, and identities. We help children develop the language to notice and challenge unfairness.

Welcoming All Families

We serve families of all structures, backgrounds, and traditions. Whether you're a single parent, same-sex couple, multigenerational household, or any other configuration—you belong here. We honor and learn from the diversity of our community.

Supporting All Abilities

We welcome children with diverse developmental needs and work with families and specialists to provide appropriate support. Our teachers receive ongoing training in inclusive practices, and we adapt our environment and curriculum as needed.

Culturally Responsive Practice

We recognize that families bring valuable cultural knowledge and practices. We invite families to share traditions, languages, and celebrations. Our goal is for every child to see themselves reflected in our program.

NAEYC Accreditation

Both of our centers hold accreditation from the National Association for the Education of Young Children (NAEYC)—the gold standard in early childhood education.

NAEYC accreditation means we meet rigorous national standards for:

  • Curriculum and teaching practices
  • Health, safety, and nutrition
  • Teacher qualifications and professional development
  • Family and community partnerships
  • Physical environment and equipment
  • Leadership and management

Only about 8% of early childhood programs nationwide achieve NAEYC accreditation. When you choose Beginnings, you can trust that your child is in a program that has been independently verified to meet the highest standards.

Our History

1992

Founded as a Parent Cooperative

A small group of Queen Anne parents, frustrated with the lack of quality childcare options, came together to create the learning environment they wanted for their children. The first "Beginnings" opened in a rented church basement with 12 children.

1998

First NAEYC Accreditation

After years of building our program, we achieved NAEYC accreditation—recognition that our small cooperative had built something special. We moved into our current Queen Anne building the same year.

2005

Incorporated as 501(c)(3) Nonprofit

To ensure long-term sustainability and community accountability, we transitioned from a cooperative structure to a formal nonprofit organization while maintaining our participatory spirit.

2018

Capitol Hill Campus Opens

After years of waitlists and requests from east-side families, we opened our second location in Capitol Hill—a purpose-built facility designed from the ground up for early learning.

Today

Serving 150+ Families

We now serve over 150 children across two locations with a team of 35+ educators. Many of our current families were referred by alumni—a testament to the lasting impact of the Beginnings community.
