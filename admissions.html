Enrollment & Openings

Looking for a spot? Here's what's available right now, and how to get started. The whole process is simple—we want to make this easy for you.

Current Openings

Availability changes regularly. If your preferred program shows "Waitlist Only," we still encourage you to inquire—spots often open unexpectedly.

About Our Waitlist

Infant spots are usually waitlist-based due to high demand and small class sizes—typical wait times are 6–12 months. Toddler and preschool openings occur more frequently, especially in late summer and early fall. We encourage families to inquire early, even if you're planning ahead.

Start Your Enrollment Inquiry

How Enrollment Works

From first inquiry to first day, here's what to expect.

1

Submit an Inquiry

Fill out our simple online form with your child's information and preferred location. We'll review your inquiry and follow up within 1–2 business days to discuss availability and next steps.

2

Tour the School

Visit your preferred location to see our classrooms in action, meet teachers, observe children at play, and ask any questions you have. Tours are typically 30–45 minutes and scheduled during the school day.

3

Receive an Offer

When a spot opens for your child's age group and location, we'll contact you with an enrollment offer. You'll receive detailed information about tuition, schedules, and required paperwork. Offers are typically held for one week.

4

Start & Orientation

Before your child's first day, we'll schedule an orientation visit where you and your child can spend time in the classroom together. We'll create a personalized transition plan based on your child's needs and your family's schedule.

Who We Serve

Ages

We accept children from 3 months to 5 years old. Children typically transition to kindergarten in the fall of the year they turn 5.

Schedule

We offer full-day care (7:00 AM – 6:00 PM) five days a week. At this time, we do not offer part-time or drop-in options.

Inclusion

We welcome children of all abilities, family structures, and backgrounds. We work with families and specialists to provide accommodations and support for children with diverse needs.

Priorities

Current families receive priority for siblings. After that, we consider waitlist order, diversity of our community, and program balance.

Tuition & Fees

As a nonprofit, we work to keep tuition as affordable as possible while ensuring our teachers are fairly compensated and our programs are well-resourced.

Detailed tuition information is shared during your tour and upon receiving an enrollment offer. Tuition varies by age group (infant care is higher due to lower ratios) and is due monthly.

What's Included

  • All meals and snacks (organic, healthy options)
  • Diapers and wipes for infants and toddlers
  • Art supplies and learning materials
  • Regular developmental updates and conferences
  • Access to our family communication app

Additional Fees

  • One-time enrollment fee upon acceptance
  • Annual materials fee (covers special projects and field trips)

Financial assistance: Limited scholarship support may be available for families demonstrating need. Please ask during your tour.

Contact Us With Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How long is the typical waitlist?

Infant waitlists are typically 6–12 months. Toddler and preschool waitlists vary but are often shorter, especially if you have flexibility on start date. We recommend inquiring as early as possible.

Can I tour before submitting an inquiry?

We ask that you submit an inquiry first so we can provide you with relevant information about availability before your visit. The inquiry form is simple and takes just a few minutes.

What if I'm not sure which location I prefer?

You can indicate interest in both locations on your inquiry. Many families tour both before deciding. Our programs and philosophy are the same at each site—the main differences are the physical space and community vibe.

Do you offer part-time care?

Currently, we only offer full-time care (5 days per week). This allows children to build consistent relationships and fully participate in our curriculum.

What happens during the transition/orientation period?

We create a personalized plan for each child. Typically, this includes a parent-accompanied visit, then gradually longer periods where your child is in the classroom with teachers. We go at your child's pace.

Is there a deposit required?

Yes, upon accepting an enrollment offer, we require a non-refundable enrollment fee and a deposit equal to one month's tuition. The deposit is applied to your last month.
