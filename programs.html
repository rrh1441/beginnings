Programs Built for Every Age

What a 6-month-old needs is completely different from what a 4-year-old needs. That's why we have dedicated classrooms, trained teachers, and age-specific approaches for each stage.

Infants (3–12 mo) Toddlers (1–3 yr) Preschool (3–5 yr)

How Kids Learn Here

We skip the worksheets. Instead, children learn by doing—building, painting, asking questions, and figuring things out with their hands and their friends.

Learning Through Projects

When kids get fascinated by bugs, we spend weeks on bugs—drawing them, finding them, learning what they eat. That's how curiosity turns into real learning that sticks.

Same Teachers, Real Relationships

Your child's teacher won't change every few months. They'll know your kid's quirks, fears, and what makes them light up—and you'll know them too.

Small Groups, Big Attention

With ratios like 1:4 for infants and 1:8 for preschool, teachers can actually respond to each child—not just manage a crowd.

Rooms Designed for Kids

Low shelves they can reach. Natural materials that feel good. Cozy reading corners. Spaces that say "explore this" instead of "don't touch."

Ages 3–12 months

Infant Program

The hardest part of going back to work is leaving your baby. We make it easier.

Teacher Ratio

1:4

Group Size

8 infants

Hours

Full day

Your baby will have the same caregivers every day—people who learn their hungry cry vs. their tired cry, who know they like to be held just so, and who text you photos because they know you're thinking about them.

A Typical Day

Days follow your baby's natural rhythms. We coordinate feeding and nap schedules with your home routine, offer tummy time and sensory exploration, read books, sing songs, and provide lots of floor time for movement.

How We Support Development

Teachers respond promptly to cries and cues, narrate caregiving moments ("I'm going to pick you up now"), and offer age-appropriate sensory materials—rattles, fabric books, mirrors, and safe objects to mouth and explore.

Transitions & Naps

Infants sleep on demand in individual cribs in a quiet sleep room. We follow safe sleep practices and work closely with you to establish comfortable routines.

Parent Communication

You'll receive daily reports via our app with feeding times, diaper changes, naps, and activities. We share photos and observations throughout the day so you never feel disconnected.

Outdoor Time

Weather permitting, infants enjoy time outdoors in protected areas—feeling grass, watching leaves, and experiencing nature from strollers or blankets.

Continuity of Care

Your baby will have consistent primary caregivers who know their preferences, signals, and personality. This relationship is the foundation of secure attachment.

Ages 1–3 years

Toddler Program

They're into everything, saying new words daily, and testing every boundary. We get it.

Teacher Ratio

1:5

Group Size

10 toddlers

Hours

Full day

Toddlers need to move, touch, pour, stack, and occasionally melt down. Our classrooms are built for all of it—with patient teachers who know how to channel the chaos into painting, sensory play, early friendships, and 50 new words a week.

A Typical Day

Mornings include free play, art activities, and outdoor time. After lunch, toddlers nap in a quiet room, then enjoy afternoon snack and more exploration. The rhythm is consistent but flexible.

How We Support Development

Teachers scaffold learning through rich conversation, open-ended questions, and materials that invite discovery—blocks, water play, playdough, painting, dramatic play, and sensory bins.

Language & Communication

We immerse toddlers in language—naming objects, reading books, singing songs, and narrating activities. For children still developing speech, we use gestures and simple signs.

Social-Emotional Growth

Toddlers are learning to navigate emotions and relationships. Teachers help children name feelings, resolve conflicts gently, and develop empathy through modeling and support.

Toilet Learning

When children show signs of readiness, we partner with families on a relaxed, child-led approach to toilet learning. There's no pressure or timeline—just encouragement and patience.

Outdoor Adventures

Toddlers spend significant time outdoors every day—climbing, running, digging, and exploring nature. Our playgrounds are designed for this age with low climbers, sand areas, and natural elements.

Ages 3–5 years

Preschool Program

In two years, they'll walk into a kindergarten classroom. We make sure they're ready—and excited.

Teacher Ratio

1:8

Group Size

16 children

Hours

Full day

Your child will learn to write their name, recognize letters, count, and follow multi-step directions. But what kindergarten teachers tell us they really notice? Our kids raise their hands, take turns, and aren't afraid to try new things.

A Typical Day

Mornings include circle time, small-group project work, and outdoor play. After lunch and rest time, children engage in art, dramatic play, and choice activities. The day ends with stories and reflection.

Project-Based Learning

Children dive deep into topics that fascinate them—insects, construction, mapping their neighborhood, or how things grow. Projects unfold over weeks, integrating literacy, math, science, and art.

Emergent Literacy

Through read-alouds, storytelling, letter exploration, and meaningful writing experiences, children develop the foundations of reading. We follow children's lead rather than drilling isolated skills.

Math & Scientific Thinking

Counting, measuring, sorting, patterning, and experimenting happen naturally through play. Teachers extend children's thinking with questions like "How could we find out?" and "What do you notice?"

Social-Emotional Skills

Preschoolers practice self-regulation, problem-solving, and collaboration. Teachers support children in navigating friendships, expressing feelings, and working through disagreements constructively.

Kindergarten Readiness

Children leave our program confident, curious, and socially skilled—ready for the structure and expectations of elementary school. We focus on dispositions (persistence, curiosity, flexibility) as much as skills.

See It for Yourself

The best way to know if we're right for your family is to visit. Tours take about 30 minutes.
